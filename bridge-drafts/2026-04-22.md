# Stretch isn't a property, it's a behavior

**Date:** 2026-04-22 (Wed)
**Category:** ARTICLE_TIE_IN
**Status:** drafted

## Post

stretch isn't a property. it's a behavior.

every fabric has 3 stretch directions: warp (along the length, usually stiffest), weft (across the width, looser), and bias (45 degrees, the most stretch).

a "12% stretch" spec sheet usually means warp. weft might be 4%. bias could be 30%+.

this matters because clothing, bags, and softgoods rarely pull along just one direction. a strap loaded at an angle is pulling on the bias. a curved seam crosses all three.

if you design assuming uniform stretch, your prototype will look right and your production run will warp at the seams.

rule of thumb: get the warp/weft/bias numbers from your supplier before you draw a single panel.
