# April 24, 2026 - Seam Allowance

**Category:** MFG_HOT_TAKE

## X Post (~280 char)

every cad model of a softgoods product is wrong by 10mm. that's seam allowance - the fabric tucked inside every stitch. cad shows the finished shape. production needs the cut shape, which is bigger. most designers learn this when their first prototype comes back too small. your tech pack needs allowance baked in from the start. 10mm for straight stitches, 6mm for binding tape, 3mm for overlock.

## LinkedIn Post

Every CAD model of a softgoods product is wrong by 10mm.

That's seam allowance - the fabric tucked inside every stitch. CAD shows the finished shape. Production needs the cut shape, which is bigger.

Most designers learn this when their first prototype comes back too small.

Your tech pack needs allowance baked in from the start. 10mm for straight stitches, 6mm for binding tape, 3mm for overlock.
