# Seam allowance: where beautiful designs die in production

**Date:** 2026-04-24 (Fri)
**Category:** MFG_HOT_TAKE
**Status:** drafted

## Post

every cad model of a softgoods product is wrong by 10mm.

that's seam allowance. the extra fabric tucked inside every stitch line that holds the seam together. cad shows the finished outer shape. production needs the cut shape, which is bigger.

most designers learn this when their first prototype comes back too small. the curve they spent a week on now sits 1cm inside the strap. the angle they cared about is consumed by the binding.

your tech pack needs allowance baked in from the start. 10mm for straight stitches, 6mm for binding tape, 3mm for overlock. nest them in your pattern before you cut, not after.

beautiful designs don't die in cad. they die in the stitch line.
