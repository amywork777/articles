## LinkedIn Post

"Design for manufacturing" is taught wrong.

It's not a checklist you run at the end of a project. It's not a review gate before release. It's a habit of imagining the person who has to make your part while you draw every single line.

If you've never watched a machinist struggle to hold your part in a fixture, you're not designing for manufacturing. You're designing for renders.

The best DFM education isn't a course. It's a week on a factory floor watching your beautiful CAD model become someone else's nightmare.
