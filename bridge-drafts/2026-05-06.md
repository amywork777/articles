# Bondline thickness: the hidden spec

**Date:** 2026-05-06 (Wed)
**Category:** MFG_HOT_TAKE
**Status:** drafted

## Post

every bonded assembly has a specification nobody puts on the drawing: bondline thickness.

it's the gap between two parts that gets filled with adhesive. too thin (under 0.05mm) and the bond is brittle and starves the glue. too thick (over 0.5mm) and the cure shrinks, the joint weakens, the part warps.

the right number depends on the adhesive: epoxy likes 0.1-0.25mm. acrylic likes 0.05-0.1mm. silicones can run thicker.

most cad models show two parts touching. production has to invent the gap.

if you care about the bond, dimension the gap.
