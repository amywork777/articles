## LinkedIn Post

Every time someone says "vegan leather" I check the spec sheet.

9 times out of 10, it's polyurethane—a petroleum-based plastic with a 500-year decomposition timeline. You didn't save a cow. You made more plastic.

"Vegan leather" is a marketing term, not a materials category. Real alternatives exist (mycelium, cactus, pineapple leaf) but they're expensive, hard to scale, and rarely what brands are actually shipping.

If you care about sustainability, read the materials list. If it says PU or PVC, you're buying plastic with better branding.
