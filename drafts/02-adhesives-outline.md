## The split: thermosets vs thermoplastics
- **Thermosets** (epoxies, urethanes, cyanoacrylates): cure via chemical cross-linking, irreversible, high strength, brittle, temperature-stable
- **Thermoplastics** (hot melts, PSAs): melt and re-melt, fast cycle times, lower temp ceiling, more compliant
- When to use which:
  - thermosets for structural + high-temp
  - thermoplastics for assembly speed, repositioning, softgoods lamination
- Failure modes: thermosets fail brittle, thermoplastics creep
- Cure chemistry matters more than data sheet strength

## Surface prep + application
- Surface energy: why teflon and silicone won't bond to anything without treatment
- Plasma vs corona vs chemical etch
- Contamination tolerance: thermosets picky, hot melts forgive more
- Bondline thickness: thicker isn't stronger, there's an optimum

## Design considerations
- Joint geometry > adhesive choice (lap shear vs peel vs cleavage)
- You want loads in shear, never peel
- CTE mismatch tears bonds apart over thermal cycles even if static strength looks fine
- Accelerated aging: humidity + thermal cycling + UV

## Why this matters now
- Adhesives are replacing screws everywhere (Apple, Dyson, EV battery packs)
- Design tooling is a PDF data sheet and a tech rep's phone number
- No real CAD integration - you can't simulate a bondline the way you can a bolted joint
